# 🎯 Splitting Without Splitting: The Art of Scaling Teams Wisely

I had an insightful conversation at #AgileMeetsArchitecture with Tsvetelina Plummer and Pricillia Gunawan, and I had to share this: **the conventional wisdom about splitting large teams might be incomplete.**

Here's the challenge: You have a 25-person team with diverse specialized roles (frontend, backend, QA, data scientists, MLOps, etc.). The obvious solution? Split them. But what if splitting actually creates MORE problems than it solves?

🔍 **Key Insights from the episode:**

✨ **1. Splitting Without Splitting** - Instead of hard team splits, create spaces for deep work WITHIN a cohesive unit while maintaining shared culture, values, and principles.

✨ **2. The 3 Forces Framework** - Every organizational decision isn't just about architecture or product. It's equally about the **people**. How they interact, who wants to collaborate, and who needs stability matters.

✨ **3. Fluid Team Dynamics** - One team runs 3 parallel backlogs (business operations, data science research, engineering standards) and fluidly re-teams each sprint based on priorities. Yes, it's unconventional. Yes, it works.

✨ **4. Visualization as Strategy** - Communication maps, stakeholder diagrams, and architecture blueprints aren't just documentation — they're decision-making tools that reveal patterns you can't see in your head.

✨ **5. Context Over Dogma** - Different teams need different solutions. One team uses SAFe-like patterns, another embraces LeSS principles, another does something completely custom. No one-size-fits-all.

🎬 **The real talk:** These coaches show how 25+ person teams CAN work effectively —  by consciously designing for collaboration, trust, and deep work zones.
