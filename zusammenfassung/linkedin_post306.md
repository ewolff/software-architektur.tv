# From Startup to Scale: How CircleK's eMobility Team Mastered Cross-Functional Alignment 🚀

Scaling a startup from 5 people to a full-fledged organization is messy. CircleK's eMobility journey is proof. We spoke with Eduardo da Silva and Guro Fladvad Størdal at the Agile meets Architecture conference.

In this episode, we dive deep into how a team navigated the chaos of rapid growth—from exploring whether EV charging even made business sense (2018) to expanding across Scandinavia and beyond.

**Key takeaways:**

🔄 **Team Topologies is important** – But it works best when combined with Domain-Driven Design and Wardley Mapping. Using one without the others? You'll miss critical insights.

🎯 **Undefined teams signal pain** – Some teams were struggeling. The breakthrough came when they named it: "undefined teams with undefined interactions" = constant coordination = slow delivery. Naming the problem was half the solution.

🏗️ **Your architecture reflects your organization** – Big ball of mud? Blame unclear vision and misaligned teams, not just poor coding decisions.

⚡ **The Architectural Modernization Enabling Team** – How mixing external consultants with internal stakeholders (including senior leadership) became the force multiplier for genuine change.

💡 **It takes time. Real time.** – It tooks month with lots of different workshops. But they made the problems visible, celebrated wins early, and kept momentum.

**What you'll learn:**
- Running effective listening sessions 
- Balancing build-vs-buy decisions with Wardley Mapping
- How to make organizational change visible and believable

This isn't theory. It's a real case study of messy, beautiful, organizational transformation.
